About Me

Steffany Bahamon
Technology Educator & Software Engineer
Chicago, IL | Miami roots | Colombian heritage

Background

I'm a Colombian-American technologist and educator who bridges cultures, languages, and technical expertise. Growing up in Miami gave me a deep connection to Latin American culture and the warmth of tropical waters. For the past ten years, Chicago has been home—where Lake Michigan's cool depths have taught me resilience through Midwest winters and inspired my appreciation for the city's rich architectural and cultural history.

My career in technology has been driven by a single mission: making complex technical concepts accessible to everyone. I've designed and delivered over 600 classes across cybersecurity, data analysis, and emerging technologies—building curriculum from scratch, measuring learner outcomes, and continuously iterating on what works. My audiences have ranged from complete beginners to experienced professionals, and I teach in both English and Spanish, ensuring that language is never a barrier to building real skills.

Professional Focus

My work sits at the intersection of education, technology, and program design. I specialize in:

  • Learning Program Design: Building curriculum and training programs from the ground up—designing learning paths, creating assessments, and measuring outcomes to ensure real skill development
  • Technical Education: Translating complex concepts in cybersecurity, AI, and data analysis into practical, engaging learning experiences for diverse skill levels
  • AI & Security: Exploring how Large Language Models and AI tools are transforming security operations, threat detection, and how we work and learn—from incident response to knowledge management
  • Bilingual & Cross-Cultural Instruction: Delivering technical education in English and Spanish, building inclusive learning environments that serve diverse communities

Beyond Work

When I'm not teaching or building systems, you'll find me:

  • Following Formula 1: The perfect blend of engineering excellence, strategy, and competition
  • Exploring Chicago: From architecture boat tours to discovering the history behind the city's neighborhoods and skyline
  • Travel Planning: Always plotting the next adventure, whether it's exploring new cities or revisiting favorite places
  • Learning: Constantly curious about new technologies, security research, adult learning, and ways to improve how we teach and learn
