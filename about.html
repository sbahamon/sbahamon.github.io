About Me

Steffany Bahamon
Cyber Defense Educator & Software Engineer
Chicago, IL | Miami roots | Colombian heritage

Background

I'm a Colombian-American cybersecurity professional who bridges cultures, languages, and technical expertise. Growing up in Miami gave me a deep connection to Latin American culture and the warmth of tropical waters. For the past ten years, Chicago has been home—where Lake Michigan's cool depths have taught me resilience through Midwest winters and inspired my appreciation for the city's rich architectural and cultural history.

My journey in cybersecurity has been driven by a mission: making security accessible to everyone. I've delivered over 600 classes, teaching cybersecurity fundamentals to diverse audiences—from complete beginners to experienced professionals looking to level up their skills. I teach in both English and Spanish, ensuring that language is never a barrier to learning how to protect what matters.

Professional Focus

My work sits at the intersection of education, cybersecurity, and software engineering. I specialize in:

  • Cybersecurity Education: Developing and delivering engaging, practical curriculum that bridges theory and real-world application
  • Threat Detection & Defense: Building and implementing security systems that actually work in production environments
  • AI & Security: Exploring how Large Language Models and AI tools are transforming security operations, threat detection, and incident response
  • Bilingual Instruction: Teaching technical concepts in English and Spanish to serve diverse communities

Beyond Work

When I'm not teaching or building systems, you'll find me:

  • Following Formula 1: The perfect blend of engineering excellence, strategy, and competition
  • Exploring Chicago: From architecture boat tours to discovering the history behind the city's neighborhoods and skyline
  • Travel Planning: Always plotting the next adventure, whether it's exploring new cities or revisiting favorite places
  • Learning: Constantly curious about new technologies, security research, and ways to improve how we teach and learn
